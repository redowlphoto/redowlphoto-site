On a quiet winter day in North Jutland, a solitary oak tree stands alone in a snow-covered landscape, separated from its surroundings by fog and silence.

The muted winter light and the reduced palette strip the scene down to its essentials, allowing form, balance, and negative space to define the image. The tree becomes a point of stillness, holding its ground as the landscape dissolves into softness.

This work explores themes of solitude, restraint, and Nordic minimalism, where winter removes distraction and leaves only structure, atmosphere, and presence.
